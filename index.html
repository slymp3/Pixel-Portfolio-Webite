About RuneSprites

RuneSprites is a digital art collection featuring 2007 unique Ordinal NFTs (non-fungible tokens) inscribed on the Bitcoin blockchain. Each RuneSprite draws inspiration from the nostalgic armor, items, and characters found in the iconic game RuneScape. Born out of a profound admiration for RuneScape's legacy, our mission is to immortalize its essence through meticulously crafted sprite characters.

Every RuneSprite is a unique representation, intricately woven into the fabric of the Bitcoin blockchain. With its fusion of nostalgic charm and blockchain technology, RuneSprites offers collectors a timeless treasure that will endure forever. Each collectible features its own distinct blend of backgrounds, colors, and items, making it a rare and coveted digital masterpiece.

These RuneSprites are powered by Bitcoin and are inscribed on the blockchain, ensuring their authenticity and permanence. Featuring procedurally generated art, they encapsulate early 2000's video game nostalgia, evoking fond memories for fans of RuneScape while offering a new dimension of digital art appreciation.

What are RuneSprites?

RuneSprites is a collection of RuneScape-inspired Ordinals inscriptions hosted on the Bitcoin blockchain. With a deep-rooted love for RuneScape, RuneSprites aims to immortalize its essence through meticulously crafted sprite characters. The collection contains 2007 randomly computer-generated RuneSprites.

Why did RuneSprites use the Bitcoin Ordinals protocol?

RuneSprites utilizes the Bitcoin Ordinals protocol to mint NFTs directly onto the Bitcoin blockchain, providing each satoshi with a unique identity. This protocol ensures the authenticity and permanence of RuneSprites by embedding them directly into the Bitcoin blockchain, enhancing marketability and desirability.

The Ordinals protocol assigns unique IDs to each satoshi, recording them on the Bitcoin blockchain to verify authenticity and ensure the correct Bitcoin ordinal series. This process makes RuneSprites immutable and resistant to modification.
